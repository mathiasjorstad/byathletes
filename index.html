Lewis Hamilton Is Betting on What Comes After the Podium

From Formula 1 to non-alcoholic spirits and film production — Hamilton's off-track empire is accelerating faster than anything he's done behind the wheel.

By Elena Rossi  ·  Staff Writer

Lewis Hamilton has won seven Formula 1 World Championships. But the business he's building off the track might outlast all of them. In 2022 he co-founded Almave, a non-alcoholic agave spirits brand developed with Mexican master distiller Casa Lumbre — positioning himself at the intersection of luxury wellness and sustainable agriculture before either trend hit the mainstream.

Almave closed a $20M Series A in March 2026, backed by LVMH Ventures and Talis Capital, placing the brand's valuation at $85M. Distribution now covers 18 countries with listings in Harrods, Selfridges, Galeries Lafayette, and 400+ Michelin-starred restaurants. The brand moved 1.2M units in 2025 — its first full commercial year.

Alongside Almave, Hamilton's Dawn Apollo Films has three projects in post-production with streaming commitments totalling £30M. He holds equity positions in three European tech startups through his family office. The infrastructure is in place — Hamilton is playing a 20-year game.

"Formula 1 gave me discipline. Business gave me freedom. You study the competition, you build a system, and then you execute."

— Lewis Hamilton, Co-Founder, Almave

Startup Tips

The athlete's
startup playbook

01

Build in Europe, scale globally from day one

European athletes have access to world-class talent, EU market scale, and institutional capital hungry for founder-led brands. Don't limit your ambition to your home market at launch.

02

Turn training discipline into operating systems

The consistency that made you elite is a superpower in business. Document your standards and decision frameworks — then make your company run the same way.

03

The brand is the moat, not the product

Products can be copied. A brand built on genuine personal conviction is almost impossible to replicate. Lead with your story before you lead with your SKUs.

04

Hire your weaknesses before your strengths

You know what you're good at. Your first hires should cover your blind spots — finance, operations, product — not add more of what you already bring.

05

Understand unit economics before raising capital

Know your customer acquisition cost, lifetime value, and gross margin before any investor meeting. Your answer will set the terms of the conversation.

06

Use your playing career as proof-of-concept

While you're still competing, test ideas with minimum viable products and real customers. Your profile reduces friction — but the market will still tell you the truth.

07

Own the relationship with your customer

Email lists and direct commerce channels are assets you own. Social media reach is borrowed. Build the infrastructure to reach customers without a platform's permission.
