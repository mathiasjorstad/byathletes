Athlete Spotlight

From the penalty box
to the boardroom

After 14 NHL seasons, Marcus Reil didn't slow down — he built a $40M recovery-tech startup that's changing how elite athletes heal.

By Jordan Falk  ·  Staff Writer

When Marcus Reil took his last shift in the 2021 playoffs, he already had a term sheet in his pocket. While teammates celebrated, Reil was quietly on the phone with a seed investor backing his next play — a recovery-tech platform built by someone who had lived every mile of the pain.

"Discipline, pain tolerance, team-building — that's not just sport. That's the startup playbook."

— Marcus Reil, Founder & CEO, IceForm Health

IceForm Health combines wearable biosensors with AI-driven recovery protocols. Today, 600+ elite athletes across 12 leagues use the platform. In March 2026, it closed a $40M Series B led by a16z's sports-tech fund.

Next up: European expansion into Bundesliga clubs, and a Series C to bring IceForm to collegiate sports by 2027.

Startup Tips

The athlete's
startup playbook

01

Your sport is your unfair advantage

Build in the industry you know. Your credibility and network in sport is a moat no outsider can replicate.

02

Treat your cap table like your roster

Only bring on investors who add value beyond capital. Dead weight costs you the game.

03

Hire a CFO before you think you need one

Athletes are trained to push through pain. That instinct is lethal in business finance.

04

Your platform is your first marketing channel

Your audience already trusts you. Build for them first — scale second.

05

Start building during your playing career

The most successful athlete-founders start before retirement — when you still have income to absorb the losses.

06

Failure is just film review

In sport, you watch the tape when you lose. A failed launch is data. Run post-mortems. Improve the next attempt.

07

Find a co-founder with opposite skills

You know the sport. Find someone who knows the market, the tech, or the finance side.
