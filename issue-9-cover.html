Mbappé Is Building a Business Empire at 27

Kylian Mbappé owns a football club, runs a film production company, and controls his own image rights — all while playing for Real Madrid.

By Léa Fontaine  ·  April 10, 2026

FootballMedia & FilmClub OwnershipNext WSG€65M Portfolio

Kylian Mbappé is 27, plays for Real Madrid, and is quietly running one of the most structured athlete business operations in Europe. His holding company, Next WSG, coordinates three active ventures: KM Productions (a Paris-based content and film studio), a majority ownership stake in AS Caen — a Ligue 2 club in Normandy he acquired in 2023 for a reported €12M — and a growing equity portfolio of minority positions in European consumer and media tech.

KM Productions closed its first full production slate in 2025: six projects in development across Canal+ and Netflix Europe, with one documentary already generating €4.2M in licensing revenue. The AS Caen stake has appreciated as the club stabilised its finances and invested in youth infrastructure. Next WSG's broader portfolio is valued at an estimated €65M across 11 assets — managed by a five-person team based in Paris.

The structural move that made all of this possible came in 2022, when Mbappé renegotiated his PSG contract to retain full control over his commercial image rights — a clause estimated to be worth €30M in additional earnings over the contract period. That decision is now the operating principle behind Next WSG: own the IP, control the distribution, and build income streams that survive after the boots come off.

“The money from football funds the infrastructure. The investments are the future. I want to build things that outlast me.”

— Kylian Mbappé, Founder, Next WSG
